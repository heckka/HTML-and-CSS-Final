Flowers

Choosing flowers for your garden depend on three things.

How much Sun does it recieve, how much water/rain, and what colors do you want?

Flowers:Basic Care
Outside conditions: Sun, rainfall, and soil type need to be researched for each flower you want to plant.
Size:Find out how big the plant can get so you can make sure it has enough room.
Colors: Choose your flowers based on what they need and what you want.
Fragrance:All flowers has a smell, make sure you enjoy the scents, and also be aware of any scents that may attract unwanted creatures such as bugs or other animals. For example Catnip will attract cats.

Apples

There are many different types of Apple trees and all trees need something to crosspolinate with in order to bare fruit. A minimum of three trees are needed in order to polinate.

Fruit:Check to see what trees it needs for crosspolination
Outside Conditions: Most trees will need full sun and lots of rainfall to bare fruit.
Size: Some apple trees get much bigger than others make sure to plant them very far apart to give plenty of room to grow.
Types Honeycrisp, Gala, Granny Smith, and a few examples of types of apple trees. All trees will nee dpest control to help keep them alive and to bare fruit for you to eat. When the trees grow large prune every few years to keep them strong, and to prevent them from overtaking other trees.

Colors

Gardens have a variety of colors, and the best part you get to control that part. Some people love a variety of colors, while others want specific colors. Feel free to choose what makes you happiest. Gardens can also have themes, statues, water features, and more. Be creative, and willing to get your hands dirty!

Blue flowers:Grape hyacinth, Columbine, Corn flower, and Sage.
Pink flowers:Roses, Allium, Bleeding Heart, Camelia, and Rhododendron.
Yellow flowers:Black-eyed Susan, Marigold, Sunflower, Daffodil, and Ausmas.
Greens (no flowers):Hosta, Aloe, Ferns,Ivy, Shamrocks, and mosses.
Multicolored: Tulips, Roses, Peony, Snapdragons, Irises, and Lilys.

Herbs

Herbs are great to add to a garden or keep indoors for a small garden. Herbs can be used in cooking, tea making, and even help keep bugs away.

Cooking:Basil, Chives, Dill, Parsley, and Oregano.
Bug repelling:Mint, Citronella, Lavendar, and Lemon thyme.
Tea:Mint, Lavender, Thyme, Chamomile, and Jasmine.
